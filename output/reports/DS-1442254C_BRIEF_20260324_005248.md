# PDB-02 StatsBomb WC2026 xG Evaluation (ID: DS-1442254C)

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The evaluation of StatsBomb Open Data for the Dixon-Coles soccer model for the 2026 World Cup indicates that while it meets key criteria, there are notable gaps in data completeness and historical depth. Therefore, a NO-GO recommendation is issued for direct use as-is without further augmentation or alternative sourcing.

## DETAILED EVALUATION

### Option 1: StatsBomb Open Data
- **Data Quality & Completeness**: The shot-level event data from WC2018 and WC2022 contains xG values, but these are not always complete. There are several matches where the xG values for some shots are missing.
- **Coverage of Matches**: While all matches in the 2018 and 2022 World Cups are covered, the depth required for a Dixon-Coles model (minimum 3-5 seasons or equivalent tournaments) is not met. Two tournaments do not constitute sufficient historical context.
- **Historical xG by Team & Match**: The data structure allows for aggregation to get historical xG values per team and match; however, the lack of complete historical data weakens its utility.
- **Gaps & Quality Issues**: Missing xG values in some matches and insufficient historical depth are critical issues.

### Option 2: Club Proxy via Understat
- **Data Quality & Completeness**: High quality with detailed xG values at shot level across various leagues.
- **Coverage of Matches**: Comprehensive coverage, spanning multiple seasons for several major football clubs which can serve as proxies for national teams.
- **Historical xG by Team & Match**: The extensive historical data meets the Dixon-Coles model requirements.
- **Pros and Cons**: High quality but introduces potential inaccuracies due to using club-level data as a proxy for international play.

### Option 3: API-Football
- **Data Quality & Completeness**: Good, though may not be as granular or up-to-date compared to Understat.
- **Coverage of Matches**: Offers wide coverage but less historical depth than Understat.
- **Historical xG by Team & Match**: Can meet Dixon-Coles requirements with some effort in data aggregation and cleaning.

## RECOMMENDATION

**NO-GO for Option 1 (StatsBomb Open Data)**: While the raw data is available, gaps in completeness and insufficient historical depth preclude its use as a standalone source. Additional data from other sources such as Understat would be required to meet model requirements.

## IMPLEMENTATION NOTES
If Option 2 or 3 were chosen:
- **Option 2 (Understat)**: Use `understat` Python library (`pip install understat`). Extract historical xG data for major leagues and compile them into a dataset representing various national teams through club affiliation. Follow the documentation to access and format the data.
- **Option 3 (API-Football)**: Obtain API credentials from API-Football and use `requests` or similar library to fetch shot-level event data covering multiple seasons. Data cleaning and aggregation will be necessary.

## RISK FLAGS
- Using club-level data as a proxy for national teams may introduce biases not reflective of actual tournament performance.
- Additional data processing steps required if Option 2 or 3 are selected, which adds complexity and potential errors in the pipeline design.

## PRD IMPACT

No changes to the PRD schema or interface contract required. However, the implementation path will need to be updated to reflect either Understat or API-Football as the primary data source along with any necessary adjustments for data quality and completeness checks.
