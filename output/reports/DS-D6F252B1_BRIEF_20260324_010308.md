## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The evaluation of StatsBomb Open Data for the Dixon-Coles soccer model for WC2026 suggests that it meets most requirements with some caveats. Option 1, using StatsBomb data, is recommended as long as gaps in tournament coverage and minor xG calculation inconsistencies are addressed. Options 2 (Understat club proxy) and 3 (API-Football) were considered but found to have insufficient depth or reliability.

## DETAILED EVALUATION

### Option 1: StatsBomb Open Data
- **Data Quality and Completeness**: The shot-level event data from WC2018 and WC2022 is generally of high quality with comprehensive xG values. However, some inconsistencies in xG calculations were noted.
- **Coverage**: There are gaps in match coverage for both tournaments, particularly for less prominent teams or matches not considered key events (e.g., early round matches).
- **Dixon-Coles Input Requirements**: The data meets the requirement for historical shot-level xG by team and match. However, the missing tournament matches reduce the reliability of the model’s predictions.
- **Quality Issues**: Minor inconsistencies in how xG values are computed across different games can introduce noise into the model.

### Option 2: Understat Club Proxy
- **Data Quality and Completeness**: While Understat provides comprehensive league data, there is no direct tournament data for WC2018 or WC2022.
- **Coverage**: The club-level data offers a reasonable proxy but lacks the specific context of World Cup matches which can differ significantly from regular season play.
- **Dixon-Coles Input Requirements**: Club level data could be used if we adjust for different playing styles and environments, but it does not directly meet the historical xG requirement.

### Option 3: API-Football
- **Data Quality and Completeness**: The quality of shot-level data is good but lacks depth in terms of detailed xG values.
- **Coverage**: Provides comprehensive match coverage across various leagues but limited WC-specific data.
- **Dixon-Coles Input Requirements**: Insufficient historical tournament-specific data to meet the Dixon-Coles model requirements.

## RECOMMENDATION

**GO with Option 1 (StatsBomb)**. The StatsBomb Open Data, while not perfect, is the closest fit for our requirements and offers a solid foundation for modeling WC2026 xG outcomes. Addressing gaps in tournament coverage will be crucial to ensure reliable model predictions.
