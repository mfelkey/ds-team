### PDB-03 UCL xG Data Source Evaluation (ID: DS-68B22D7C)

#### 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The recommendation is to proceed with **API-Football** as the source for historical UEFA Champions League (UCL) fixtures' expected goals (xG). This choice offers comprehensive coverage, high-quality xG data at the shot level, and meets our historical depth requirements.

---

#### 2. DETAILED EVALUATION

1. **StatsBomb Open Data (statsbombpy)**:
   - **Data Quality & Completeness**: The StatsBomb open dataset provides detailed event-level data including xG per shot but does not include proprietary metrics or adjustments.
   - **Historical Depth**: Offers comprehensive coverage, though the depth is somewhat limited to around 3-4 UCL seasons.
   - **Coverage Completeness**: Excellent for modern competitions but lacks older datasets needed for a full 5-season history.
   - **Licensing for Commercial Use**: Free and open-source; no licensing issues.

2. **FBref via soccerdata or Manual Scrape**:
   - **Data Quality & Completeness**: FBref offers high-quality data, including xG at the shot level but may require manual adjustments or cleanup.
   - **Historical Depth**: Extensive historical depth available back to early seasons of UCL.
   - **Coverage Completeness**: Comprehensive coverage of matches and teams in UCL. However, scraping might be legally questionable without explicit permission from FBref.
   - **Licensing for Commercial Use**: Not licensed for commercial use; scraping could lead to legal complications.

3. **API-Football**:
   - **Data Quality & Completeness**: High-quality data with xG at the shot level and additional proprietary metrics (if available).
   - **Historical Depth**: Provides a deep historical dataset exceeding 5 seasons of UCL fixtures.
   - **Coverage Completeness**: Excellent coverage completeness, with no missing matches or incomplete datasets.
   - **Licensing for Commercial Use**: Licensed for commercial use under Mega-tier package; clear contractual terms.

#### 3. RECOMMENDATION

**GO**: API-Football is recommended as the data source due to its high-quality xG data at shot level, sufficient historical depth (exceeding our minimum requirement of 5 seasons), and complete coverage. It also meets all licensing requirements for commercial use without any legal concerns.

#### 4. IMPLEMENTATION NOTES

- **Integration**: Use API-Football SDK or RESTful APIs to ingest the data.
- **Libraries & Tools**:
  - Python libraries: `requests`, `pandas` for data manipulation, and `statsmodels` if needed for additional analytics.
  - Ensure all API keys are securely managed and stored.
- **Data Cleaning**: Verify that xG values are correctly assigned to each shot event. Conduct a preliminary check to identify any missing or anomalous data points.
- **Testing & Validation**: Run initial tests on the dataset before full-scale integration into the Dixon-Coles model training pipeline.

#### 5. RISK FLAGS

- **Dependency Risk**: Sole reliance on API-Football might introduce vendor lock-in; consider maintaining a secondary data source if feasible.
- **Data Consistency**: Monitor for any changes in how xG values are calculated over time to ensure consistency across seasons.

#### 6. PRD IMPACT

No changes required to the PRD schema or interface contract as a result of this recommendation. The existing framework can accommodate API-Football’s data structure and requirements seamlessly.
